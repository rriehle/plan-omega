## Usage Notes

### Purpose and Importance

The Daily Log serves as the primary operational record for the vessel. It provides:

- Legal documentation of position and activities
- Weather pattern tracking for route planning
- Maintenance and fuel consumption tracking
- Crew welfare monitoring
- Threat assessment continuity

### Completion Guidelines

1. **Consistency:** Complete at the same time each day to ensure 24-hour periods
2. **Accuracy:** Record actual observations, not estimates
3. **Detail:** Include enough detail for someone not present to understand events
4. **Objectivity:** Record facts, not opinions, especially regarding crew welfare

### Key Sections

**Position & Navigation:** Critical for passage tracking and emergency response
**Weather Conditions:** Essential for pattern recognition and forecasting
**Vessel Status:** Early problem detection through daily monitoring
**Crew Welfare:** Identifies developing morale or health issues
**Threat Assessment:** Maintains situational awareness continuity

### Storage and Backup

- Keep physical copies in waterproof storage
- Photograph/scan pages regularly for digital backup
- Consider carbon copy books for redundancy
- Store completed logs in secure, dry location

### Legal Considerations

Daily logs may be required for:

- Insurance claims
- Accident investigations
- Customs/immigration verification
- Passage documentation

Ensure entries are:

- Made in permanent ink
- Not altered after the fact
- Signed by responsible officer
- Stored for minimum 3 years
