**Frequency:** Weekly quick checks, monthly detailed inspection, quarterly comprehensive audit
**Retention:** Current audit plus 12 months historical

## Usage Guidelines

### Audit Frequency

**Weekly Quick Check (30 minutes):**

- Visual hull inspection at waterline
- Engine oil and coolant check
- Battery voltage check
- Bilge inspection
- Safety equipment count

**Monthly Detailed Inspection (2-3 hours):**

- All sections with standard thoroughness
- Test all systems under load
- Check all fluid levels
- Update provision inventory

**Quarterly Comprehensive Audit (Full day):**

- Complete detailed inspection of all systems
- Service items due (winches, windlass, etc.)
- Inspect areas rarely checked (chain locker, lazarettes)
- Update all documentation
- Review and update spare parts inventory

**Pre-Departure Audit:**

- Complete before any significant passage
- Focus on weather-critical systems
- Verify all safety equipment
- Top off all consumables

### Critical vs Non-Critical Issues

**Critical (Immediate Action Required):**

- Structural damage to hull or rig
- Engine won't start or overheats
- Steering system problems
- Navigation equipment failure
- Safety equipment deficiencies
- Significant leaks

**Non-Critical (Can Wait for Port):**

- Minor cosmetic issues
- Convenience items not working
- Spare equipment needs replacement
- Non-essential electronics
- Minor leaks in non-critical systems

### System-Specific Intervals

**Daily Checks (Underway):**

- Engine fluids before start
- Bilge morning and evening
- Battery voltage
- Rigging tension (visual)

**After Heavy Weather:**

- Complete rigging inspection
- Hull and deck inspection
- Check for chafe
- Verify all securing

**Haul-Out Items:**

- Through-hull service
- Cutlass bearing check
- Bottom paint assessment
- Anode replacement

### Documentation Best Practices

1. **Photo Documentation:**
   - Take photos of any issues found
   - Before/after for repairs
   - Serial numbers of new equipment

2. **Maintenance Tracking:**
   - Log all work performed
   - Track parts used
   - Note who performed work
   - Keep receipts

3. **Trend Analysis:**
   - Compare audits over time
   - Identify recurring issues
   - Plan preventive maintenance
   - Budget for replacements

### Integration with Other Systems

- **Maintenance Schedule:** Use findings to update maintenance calendar
- **Inventory Tracking:** Update spare parts needs
- **Passage Planning:** Consider vessel condition in route planning
- **Drill Planning:** Test equipment during drills
- **Budget Planning:** Project costs based on audit findings

### Red Flags Requiring Immediate Action

1. **Rig:** Any crack in chainplate, turnbuckle, or mast
2. **Hull:** Water ingress above normal
3. **Steering:** Any play or hard spots
4. **Engine:** Metal in oil, coolant in oil
5. **Electrical:** Burning smell, warm connections
6. **Safety:** Expired or missing critical equipment

### Record Keeping

- Keep completed audits for insurance purposes
- Digital copies in cloud storage
- Paper copies in waterproof storage
- Share with crew for awareness
- Review before major passages
